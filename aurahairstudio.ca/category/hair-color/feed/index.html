Soft Espresso Brunette

Rich brunette tones are dominating 2026, but with refinement. Soft espresso blends deep brown with subtle mocha and chocolate undertones to create movement and shine.

Unlike flat dark dye, espresso brunette incorporates micro-dimension. Fine ribbons of slightly lighter brown prevent heaviness and add natural reflection under light.

  • Grows out softly

  • Maintains shine with gloss treatments

Golden Butter Blonde

Cool platinum is no longer the default blonde. In 2026, warmth returns in the form of golden butter blonde. This shade combines creamy yellow, beige, and soft honey tones for a balanced brightness.

Golden butter blonde softens the complexion and adds radiance without looking brassy. It complements neutral and warm undertones beautifully.

  • Toning appointments every 6 to 8 weeks

  • Bond-repair support after lightening

Blonde transformations should prioritize hair integrity. Controlled lifting and professional bond protection prevent breakage and dullness.

Cherry Cola Red

Red is stronger and richer in 2026. Cherry cola blends burgundy, deep red, and brown undertones for a multidimensional finish.

This shade avoids the overly bright copper look. Instead, it delivers depth and richness that reflect beautifully under light.

Ideal for:

  • Fair to medium skin tones
  • Clients wanting a bold yet wearable shift
  • Brunettes seeking change without fa ull blonde transition

Red tones fade faster than neutral shades. Sulphate-free shampoo and colour-safe conditioners help preserve vibrancy longer.

What Affects the Cost of Hair Colouring?

Understanding what shapes the final price helps you plan your visit better. Colour work is not a one-size service; your hair history and colour goals influence everything from product usage to timing.

1. Hair Length and Thickness

Long or thick hair uses more colour and takes more time to process. This increases both product use and application time, which raises the final cost. Shorter or finer hair usually falls at the lower end of the price range.

2. Current Hair Condition

If your hair has previous colour, dryness, or breakage, your stylist may recommend strengthening treatments such as moisture or bond repair. This keeps your hair healthy during the colouring process and helps the shade last longer.

3. The Type of Colour Service

Each service requires a different level of detail. A full colour is direct and even, while highlights or balayage involve more steps and precision work. More steps require more expertise and time.

4. Desired Lightness or Depth

Lifting dark hair to a light blonde usually needs several stages to protect the hair. Adding depth or subtle tone shifts often needs less time and product.

5. Level of Maintenance You Want

Some shades require regular touch-ups. Others grow out softly and stretch your appointment timeline. During your consultation, your stylist can help you choose a colour that matches the level of upkeep you prefer.
