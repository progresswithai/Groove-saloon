Why Skin Tone Matters

Hair colour that works with your skin tone can improve the appearance of your complexion, highlight your eyes, and create overall visual harmony. When there’s a disconnect between tone and colour, even high-quality dye jobs can fall flat. Getting it right starts with understanding your natural undertone.

Step One: Identify Your Skin’s Undertone

Skin tone and skin undertone are different. Your undertone is the subtle hue beneath the surface of your skin, and it generally falls into one of three categories:

  • Warm undertones: yellow, golden, or peachy hues
  • Cool undertones: pink, red, or bluish hues
  • Neutral undertones: a blend of warm and cool characteristics

Quick ways to determine your undertone:

  • Check your veins: greenish veins suggest a warm undertone, bluish or purple indicate cool, and a mix points to neutral.
  • Observe which jewelry suits you best: gold tends to complement warm tones; silver flatters cool tones. If both look equally good, you’re likely neutral.

Hair Colour Options by Undertone

Once you’ve identified your undertone, the next step is choosing colours that complement it. Hair shades interact with your skin’s natural warmth or coolness, either balancing it out or drawing attention to key features. Matching colour to undertone isn’t about limitations; it’s about highlighting what works best for you.

For Cool Undertones

Colours with blue or violet bases often work best. These shades add contrast and reduce redness in the skin.

  • Ash brown
  • Platinum or icy blonde
  • Cool beige
  • Jet black
  • Burgundy

Avoid overly golden or brassy tones, which can amplify red tones in the skin.

For Warm Undertones

Colours with golden, copper, or warm brown bases enhance warmth in the skin and create a sun-kissed look.

  • Honey blonde
  • Caramel
  • Golden brown
  • Strawberry blonde
  • Copper red

Stay away from overly ashy shades that may dull your complexion.

For Neutral Undertones

You can wear a broad spectrum of shades, though it’s helpful to lean slightly warm or cool based on your preferences and wardrobe.

  • Soft brown
  • Rose gold
  • Espresso
  • Sandy blonde

Extremely vivid or icy tones may overshadow your natural balance.

Eye Colour and Natural Hair Matter Too

While your undertone is the core foundation for choosing colour, your eye colour and original hair shade add valuable direction. Eye colour often influences which tones bring out contrast or harmony in your features.

For instance, warm tones like honey and caramel can make hazel or brown eyes appear brighter and more golden. On the other hand, cool tones like ash or platinum enhance the clarity of blue and grey eyes, adding definition and depth.

Your starting hair colour also shapes your colouring journey. Transitioning from dark to light (or vice versa) impacts the process. Lightening very dark hair typically requires several appointments to achieve an even tone without compromising hair health. Natural pigment density can also affect how well your chosen colour holds over time, which is why a consultation is key.

Factoring in these physical traits helps your stylist create a custom result that blends naturally and highlights your best features.

Types of Colour Services

Depending on your goals, different approaches may be more suitable:

  • Permanent colour offers full coverage and is long-lasting.
  • Semi-permanent colour fades gradually and is ideal for subtle changes.
  • Highlights, lowlights, and balayage add dimension and depth without full coverage.

Caring for Your Colour

Maintaining your hair after colouring is essential for longevity and health. We recommend:

  • Sulphate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Regular deep conditioning treatments
  • Minimal heat styling and use of heat protectant when necessary
  • Scheduling touch-ups and toner refreshers every 6 to 8 weeks

These steps help preserve tone, shine, and strength between salon visits.
