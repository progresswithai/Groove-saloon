Hair is one of the first things people notice, and it can dramatically enhance or detract from your facial features. A cut that complements your face shape enhances your bone structure, balances proportions, and boosts confidence.

Whether you’re going for something bold or timeless, understanding your face shape is the first step toward your best look.

Why Face Shape Matters

Your face shape affects how a haircut frames your features. The right cut can highlight your cheekbones, soften a jawline, or add length and dimension. The wrong one? It can make your face appear wider, longer, or out of proportion.

Think of your face as the canvas and your haircut as the frame. The goal is harmony. Different shapes call for different techniques — layers, bangs, angles, or volume placement — that balance your facial proportions and direct attention where you want it most.

Aura’s stylists are trained to assess face shapes and recommend styles that bring out the best in each client. We don’t use a one-size-fits-all approach. Instead, we tailor each cut to enhance your unique beauty.

If you’re seeking a dramatic transformation or a subtle update, understanding your face shape is key. Here’s how we approach cuts for every shape.

1. Oval Face Shape

Characteristics: Balanced proportions, slightly wider cheekbones, gently tapered jawline.

Best Haircuts:

  • Almost anything goes. Oval faces are the most versatile. Whether you want long layers, a chic bob, or edgy pixie, this shape can handle it all.
  • Long waves or blunt bobs keep things modern while enhancing the natural symmetry.
  • Curtain bangs add softness without shortening the face.

Avoid: Overly heavy bangs that conceal your natural balance.

2. Round Face Shape

Characteristics: Full cheeks, rounded jawline, similar width and height.

Best Haircuts:

  • Long layers with face-framing angles help elongate the face.
  • Asymmetrical bobs or lobs (long bobs) add edge and length.
  • Side-swept bangs break up the circular shape and add dimension.

Styling Tip: Add volume at the crown and keep the sides sleek.

Avoid: Blunt cuts at the chin or straight-across bangs that accentuate roundness.

3. Square Face Shape

Characteristics: Strong, angular jawline with a broad forehead.

Best Haircuts:

  • Soft, textured layers or curls that offset sharp angles.
  • Long side-swept bangs or wispy fringes soften the brow and jaw.
  • Shoulder-length cuts with waves create movement.

Styling Tip: Round-brushing or curling can help soften harsh lines.

Avoid: Straight blunt bobs or boxy styles that emphasize squareness.

4. Heart-Shaped Face

Characteristics: Wider forehead, high cheekbones, narrow or pointed chin.

Best Haircuts:

  • Chin-length bobs or wavy lobs add width to the lower face.
  • Side-parted styles or sweeping bangs balance the forehead.
  • Pixie cuts with side volume highlight the cheekbones.

Styling Tip: Focus on volume near the jaw to balance the narrow chin.

Avoid: Short layers that add volume at the crown and taper too quickly.

5. Long or Rectangular Face Shape

Characteristics: Face is longer than it is wide, often with a long chin or forehead.

Best Haircuts:

  • Long waves or shoulder-length layers break up length.
  • Blunt bobs with side or curtain bangs shorten the face visually.
  • Fringes or bangs draw attention upward and create balance.

Styling Tip: Avoid height at the crown. Horizontal volume is your friend.

Avoid: Ultra-long, straight styles that exaggerate length.

6. Diamond Face Shape

Characteristics: Narrow forehead and chin with wide cheekbones.

Best Haircuts:

  • Medium to long layers with volume around the jawline.
  • Textured pixies that open up the cheekbones.
  • Side parts and layered fringe bring softness.

Styling Tip: Tuck one side behind the ear to show off your cheekbones.

Avoid: Styles that are too tight at the sides or add volume at the widest point.

Oval: Buzz cuts, fades, or slicked-back styles.
