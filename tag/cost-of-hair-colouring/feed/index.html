What Affects the Cost of Hair Colouring?

Understanding what shapes the final price helps you plan your visit better. Colour work is not a one-size service; your hair history and colour goals influence everything from product usage to timing.

1. Hair Length and Thickness

Long or thick hair uses more colour and takes more time to process. This increases both product use and application time, which raises the final cost. Shorter or finer hair usually falls at the lower end of the price range.

2. Current Hair Condition

If your hair has previous colour, dryness, or breakage, your stylist may recommend strengthening treatments such as moisture or bond repair. This keeps your hair healthy during the colouring process and helps the shade last longer.

3. The Type of Colour Service

Each service requires a different level of detail. A full colour is direct and even, while highlights or balayage involve more steps and precision work. More steps require more expertise and time.

4. Desired Lightness or Depth

Lifting dark hair to a light blonde usually needs several stages to protect the hair. Adding depth or subtle tone shifts often needs less time and product.

5. Level of Maintenance You Want

Some shades require regular touch-ups. Others grow out softly and stretch your appointment timeline. During your consultation, your stylist can help you choose a colour that matches the level of upkeep you prefer.

Typical Price Ranges for Popular Colour Services

Pricing varies across salons in Ontario, but these ranges give you a clear idea of what to expect when you book a colour service at a professional studio.

Full Colour

A full colour applies one solid shade across your hair.

Average cost: $90 to $180

This is ideal for refreshing your natural shade, covering greys, or shifting to a warmer or cooler tone.

Partial Highlights

Partial highlights brighten the top and sides for a natural lift.

  • Average cost: $120 to $200

They blend well with most haircuts and suit clients who want brightness with low upkeep.

Full Highlights

Full highlights add brightness throughout the entire head.

  • Average cost: $160 to $260

This service involves more foils and a longer processing time.

Balayage

Balayage creates soft, blended colour that grows out gently.

  • Average cost: $180 to $300

This method takes more time because it uses freehand painting for seamless colour shifts.

Colour Correction

Correcting banding, uneven tone, or at-home colour mishaps often requires several stages.

  • Average cost: $250 to $450+

Your stylist maps out a step-by-step plan to protect your hair and restore a balanced shade.

Gloss or Toner

A toner refreshes colour, removes brassiness, and adds shine.

  • Average cost: $25 to $60

It is often used between appointments to keep the colour bright.

How to Get the Best Value from Your Hair Colour Appointment

Colouring your hair is an investment. You can extend the life of your shade and reduce future costs with simple habits.

  1. Choose shades that match your lifestyle: If you prefer fewer salon visits, blended balayage or soft highlights offer longer grow-out periods.
  2. Follow aftercare advice: Your stylist will suggest shampoos, conditioners, and treatments suited to your colour. This keeps fading low and shining high.
  3. Book refresh appointments at the right time: Most colours look their best when refreshed every 6 to 10 weeks. Balayage can stretch up to 12 to 16 weeks.
  4. Stay consistent with your stylist: Keeping a record of your hair history helps your stylist plan future sessions with precision.
